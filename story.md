# The Story Behind Our Vision-Controlled Pen Plotter

It started with a simple thought:

**“What if a machine could *see* and  actually *draw*?”**
Not just follow instructions…  
but actually take input, process it, and recreate it physically.
That idea became our **vision-controlled pen plotter robot**.

---

##  Where It Began

We didn’t start with a perfect plan..Just curiosity.We wanted to build something that wasn’t purely software or purely hardware —  
but something in between.

Something that connects:
- Computer Vision  
- Embedded Systems  
- Real-world motion
---
## Building It

This project pushed us into multiple domains at once.

We worked on:

###  Making the machine “see”
Using computer vision, we processed images and converted them into drawable paths.

### Making the machine “move”
Those paths were translated into precise stepper motor movements.

### Making it stable
We designed and built the mechanical structure, ensuring accuracy and reducing vibration.

Every part had to work together.  
If one failed, everything failed.

---

## The Hardest Part

Not coding.  
Not wiring.

But **integration**.

Getting:
- Vision → Processing → Motion  

to work seamlessly was the real challenge.
Even a tiny misalignment or delay could ruin the entire drawing.
We spent hours debugging things that seemed invisible

---

## The Moment It Worked

Then came the moment.
The pen touched the paper…  
and started drawing.Both the motors coordinated...we could actually see oru robo arm working similar to a human arm..except that we didnt keep the wrist..
Line by line, the machine recreated something we had only seen on a screen.
The toughest part was managing torque...initially our motors didnt coordinate well...thats when our seniors mention certain mistakes and us being careless about the torque..but finnaly we coordinated them!!
